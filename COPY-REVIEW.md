# Dream House Copy Review
### Through the lens of deep feedback on framing, hierarchy, and holding space

This document reviews all Dream House website copy against feedback that challenges several assumptions embedded in the current language. The feedback came from an experienced facilitator and was directed at a related project, but the critique cuts to the heart of how Dream House frames itself, its participants, and their experience.

**Format:**
- ~~Strikethrough~~ = suggested removal
- **Bold** = suggested addition
- `> [COMMENT]` = editorial reasoning
- `> [KEEP]` = why something stays unchanged
- `> [CONSIDER]` = not a firm recommendation, but worth sitting with

---

## A Note Before We Begin

The feedback raises a genuine philosophical tension: **Dream House needs to create desire** (it's an invitation) **but it also claims to hold space for people** (it's a container). These two functions pull in opposite directions. An invitation says "this is extraordinary, you want to be here." A container says "whatever you bring is welcome." The current copy leans heavily toward invitation. This review asks: can it do both?

The hardest question the feedback poses isn't about any single line of copy. It's this: **Does Dream House know what it is, or does it know what it wants to be perceived as?** The copy sometimes confuses these. When it promises specific outcomes ("your dreams become reality"), it's marketing a perception. When it describes what it actually offers ("a month, a house, a group of people, a set of intentions"), it's being honest about what it is. The honest version is, counterintuitively, more compelling.

## Landing Page

### Hero Gate

> [KEEP] The hero gate ("Dream your way in" / "How can others support your dream?") is beautiful and doesn't trigger any of the feedback concerns. It's participatory, open-ended, and doesn't prescribe what the dream should be. The act of typing your dream is intimate — it creates personal investment without hierarchy. No changes.

### Section: Story — Beach

**Current:**
- You've felt it.
- That version of yourself that's lit up, inspired, in love with being alive.
- When everything feels possible.

**Suggested:**
- You've felt it.
- That version of yourself that's ~~lit up, inspired, in love with being alive~~ **closer to something you can't quite name**.
- When ~~everything feels possible~~ **something opens up**.

> [COMMENT — Theme D: Space for varied experience] "Lit up, inspired, in love with being alive" prescribes a very specific emotional state. Many people arrive at transformational spaces from exhaustion, grief, or quiet searching — not from a memory of feeling "lit up." The vaguer "closer to something you can't quite name" invites people to project their own experience onto it, which is more inclusive and arguably more evocative. "Everything feels possible" is the same issue — it's a very specific (and manic-adjacent) emotional state. "Something opens up" is gentler and wider.

> [COUNTER-ARGUMENT] The current version is more visceral and exciting. "Lit up, inspired, in love with being alive" — most people HAVE felt this and recognize it instantly. The specificity creates emotional resonance. The suggested version is safer but may be less magnetic. **This is one where Caro should trust her gut.** If the goal is to reach people who recognize that feeling and want more of it, the current version is honest about who this is for. If the goal is wider, the softer version opens the door.

### Section: Story — Ocean

**Current:**
- Where dancing with your dreams is just a Tuesday...
- ~~Where your dreams become reality on the same day you dream them.~~
- **Where the distance between imagining and beginning seems to disappear.**
- What if that wasn't luck?

> [COMMENT — Theme B: Prescriptive outcomes] "Your dreams become reality on the same day you dream them" is the single most prescriptive line on the site. It promises a specific, extraordinary outcome. This is exactly the kind of framing the feedback warns about in point 8 — some people will experience this, some won't, some will feel they've failed if they don't. "The distance between imagining and beginning seems to disappear" keeps the magic but frames it as a *quality of the environment*, not a guaranteed personal outcome. And "seems to" adds just enough humility.

> [KEEP] "What if that wasn't luck?" — This is a question, not a claim. Questions invite reflection. This is one of the best lines on the site.

### Section: Welcome

**Current:**
- 1 month
- 10-16 people
- ~~Carefully curated~~
- **Thoughtfully gathered**
- Consciously held
- Co-created
- ~~Where inspiring people go to get inspired~~
- **Where something in you remembers what's possible**

> [COMMENT — Theme A: Hierarchy] "Carefully curated" is selection language — it implies gatekeeping, a judge deciding who's worthy. "Thoughtfully gathered" communicates the same care without the hierarchy. Dream House IS selective — but the selection is about fit, not rank. The language should reflect that.

> [COMMENT — Theme A: Hierarchy] "Where inspiring people go to get inspired" is the site's most repeated tagline and its most hierarchical. It defines attendees by whether they're "inspiring" — a quality judgment that creates an in-group. Who decides who's inspiring? The line also appears in the Principles page subtitle. Two alternatives to consider:

> **Option A** (suggested above): "Where something in you remembers what's possible" — shifts focus from external validation ("you're inspiring") to inner experience. It's also more mysterious and evocative.

> **Option B**: "Where people come to remember what they're capable of" — keeps the aspirational energy but removes the hierarchy.

> **Option C**: Keep it. It's become a brand line. It's charming. The "inspiring" label might be something people *want* to wear. Just know that it does create a subtle hierarchy, and some people will feel they need to perform "inspiring-ness" to belong.

### Section: Inspired By

> [KEEP] This entire section works beautifully and doesn't trigger the feedback concerns. It's a list of inspirations, not claims. "The surrender of vision quests" is particularly nice — it's the only mention of surrender on the entire site, which is telling.

### Section: Intelligent

**Current:**
- ~~For those that feel the need for a more intelligent way to live...~~
- **For those drawn to a more intentional way of being...**
- ... in connection with nature, ourselves and each other.
- Fertile soil for the truest version of yourself to play.

> [COMMENT — Theme C: World is broken] "A more intelligent way to live" implies the current way of living is unintelligent. It's subtly judgmental — of the participant's current life, of people who aren't seeking this. "A more intentional way of being" communicates the same aspiration without the judgment. It also shifts from "live" (external) to "being" (internal), which aligns better with the feedback's call for inner reflection rather than external critique.

> [CONSIDER — Theme D] "The truest version of yourself" assumes a fixed, knowable "true self" waiting to be uncovered. Some philosophical traditions (and the feedback's emphasis on emergence) would push back on this — the self isn't a thing to discover but a process to participate in. But this is deep philosophy and "truest version of yourself" is beautiful, widely understood, and not a hill worth dying on. Consider "a fuller version of yourself" as an alternative that implies expansion rather than uncovering — but this is a light suggestion, not a strong recommendation.

### Section: Portal

**Current:** "Every so often, life offers you a portal. Will you accept the invitation?"

> [KEEP] This is perfect. It's a question, not a claim. It positions Dream House as an *offer* that the reader chooses, not a hierarchy they're admitted into. No changes.

### Gallery Buttons

> [KEEP] "Who will be there" / "Apply Now" — functional, no concerns.

## Who Page

### Hero & Rotator Tags

**Current heading:** Who is this *for?*

**Current rotator tags:**
- The Kind / The Curious / The Mischievous / The Morally Ambitious / The Creatives / The Wonderers / The Builders / The Dreamers

> [CONSIDER — Theme A] The rotator tags categorize people into types. "The Kind" — some people will resonate, others will think "am I kind enough?" The "The [Noun]" format is inherently categorizing. But it's also visually beautiful on the banner and creates that sense of "I want to be in that group." This is a tension with no clean answer.

> **Possible softening**: Change from "The [Type]" to qualities as invitations: "The kind ones / The curious ones / The slightly mischievous" — less categorical, more warm, more "you know who you are" energy. Or leave as-is — this is a style choice more than a values choice.

### Subtitle

**Current:** This is for the ambitious weirdos & genuinely good eggs.

**Suggested:** This is for ~~the ambitious weirdos & genuinely good eggs~~ **people who take life seriously enough to be playful about it.**

> [COMMENT — Theme A] "Ambitious weirdos & genuinely good eggs" is charming and memorable, but it's tribal labeling. It creates an in-group ("I'm an ambitious weirdo!") and implicitly an out-group ("those people aren't ambitious enough / weird enough / good enough"). The feedback specifically warns about this divide being "false and not serving the work."

> [COUNTER-ARGUMENT] This line has real personality. "People who take life seriously enough to be playful about it" is less sticky, less fun. Other options: "people who care deeply and play freely" / "the kind of people who make you think and make you laugh." But honestly, "ambitious weirdos & genuinely good eggs" might be worth keeping for brand voice — just be aware it does create a boundary.

### Yes-List

**Current:**
- We accidentally inspire people, constantly
- We believe joy and ambition belong in the same room
- We share our gifts and being of service energises us
- We have something we're building that means something to us
- We miss the kind of community that was just around us earlier in life
- We get genuinely energised by people who think differently to us
- We are willing to be fully seen and see others honestly

**Suggested changes:**
- We ~~accidentally inspire people, constantly~~ **find ourselves energised by the people around us**
- We believe joy and ambition belong in the same room
- We share our gifts and being of service energises us
- We have something we're building **— or searching for —** that means something to us
- We miss the kind of community that was just around us earlier in life
- We get genuinely energised by people who think differently to us
- We are willing to be fully seen and see others honestly
- **We're comfortable with not having all the answers**

> [COMMENT — Theme A] "We accidentally inspire people, constantly" is the most hierarchical item. It presupposes that the reader *does* inspire people and knows it. Some of the most transformative people in a room are the quiet ones who don't think of themselves as inspiring. "Find ourselves energised by the people around us" shifts from "I am inspiring" to "I am receptive" — which is actually a more useful quality in a communal container.

> [COMMENT — Theme B] "We have something we're building that means something to us" — adding "or searching for" opens the door for people who are between things, in a fallow period, or deliberately not-building. The FAQ already acknowledges this ("you do not need a formal project") but the yes-list contradicts it.

> [COMMENT — Theme C/D] Adding "We're comfortable with not having all the answers" introduces the surrender/acceptance quality the feedback identifies as missing. It signals that Dream House values uncertainty alongside ambition — which is what the most mature version of this project actually believes.

### "Not For You" Section

**Current label:** A note of honesty
**Current heading:** This probably *isn't* for you if

**Suggested reframe:**

**Label:** ~~A note of honesty~~ **What to know before you apply**

**Heading:** ~~This probably *isn't* for you if~~ **This experience asks a lot**

**Current items:**
- ~~You need alcohol to have fun~~
- ~~You're looking for a conference, a vacation, or a structured curriculum~~
- ~~You need every outcome guaranteed before you arrive~~
- ~~You want to observe from the edges~~

**Suggested reframe:**
- **The house is alcohol and cigarette free for the full month**
- **There's no fixed curriculum — the shape of each day emerges from the group**
- **You won't know exactly what you're getting into. That's by design**
- **Full participation is the price of admission — this isn't a place to spectate**

> [COMMENT — Theme A: Hierarchy, reframing exclusion as honest description] The current framing tells people they're wrong: "This isn't for you IF you are [bad quality]." It's a judgment disguised as honesty. The reframed version describes what the experience actually is and lets people self-select. Same information, completely different power dynamic.

> "You need alcohol to have fun" is particularly judgmental — it implies a character flaw. "The house is alcohol and cigarette free" is a fact. People can decide for themselves whether that works for them.

> "You want to observe from the edges" assumes bad faith. Some people's way of participating IS observation, at least initially. "Full participation is the price of admission" communicates the expectation without dismissing quieter modes of engagement. (Though — "the price of admission" might be too transactional. Alternative: "This is a place for full participation, in whatever form that takes for you.")

### Be embodied

**Current:** We move, rest, eat, and breathe with intention. We live in rhythm with nature. ~~Your body knows things your mind talks you out of. This is the place where you get to listen.~~

> [KEEP] "We trust that everything we need, we already have." This is actually the most philosophically aligned with the feedback of any line on the site. ### Be a maverick

> [KEEP] "Do it your way. Be authentic, be bold. There's no template here, just you, doing the thing only you can do." This works. It's about individual expression, not hierarchy. No changes.

### Consider: A sixth principle?

> [CONSIDER — Theme 6: Surrender] All five current principles are about *doing*: dream, play, move, give, dare. The feedback identifies a missing counterweight: **being with what is.** Surrender, acceptance, not-knowing. The "Inspired By" section mentions "the surrender of vision quests" — but surrender has no principle.

> **Possible sixth principle: "Be still"** — Not everything needs to be solved, built, or transformed. Sometimes the bravest thing is to stop. To sit with what is, without needing it to be different. To trust that not-knowing is its own kind of wisdom.

> This would balance the agency of the other principles. It signals to potential participants that Dream House values presence alongside action, acceptance alongside ambition. It also directly addresses the feedback's concern (point 7) that the framing "may give people the initially lovely feeling of finding energy/agency in imposing themselves on the future" without reflecting on their participation in the present.

> **Alternative: "Be surrendered"** or **"Be present"** — though "Be still" has the most poetic weight and stands in the starkest contrast to the other principles' active energy.

## Summary of Major Themes in Changes

### 1. From "right people" to "right conditions"
The biggest shift. Instead of "you are the kind of person who belongs here," the copy should say "this is what we're creating — does it call to you?" Same selectivity, different power dynamic.

### 2. From prescribing outcomes to reporting observations
"You WILL feel X" → "We've witnessed X." "This WILL happen" → "This CAN happen." Adding "we've seen" or "often" or "some people find" creates space for varied experience without losing the aspiration.

### 3. From "the world is broken" to "here's a different rhythm"
The modern world doesn't need to be the villain for Dream House to be valuable. Removing blame ("the modern world quietly broke") and adding observation ("we've slowly drifted from") invites participants to reflect on their own participation in the patterns they want to change.

### 4. From pure agency to agency + surrender
Adding a stillness principle, softening "Everything IS possible," acknowledging that "not-knowing is its own kind of wisdom" — these create a more complete, more honest container.

### 5. From exclusion to honest description
"This isn't for you if" → "This is what the experience asks." Same boundaries, but described as qualities of the experience rather than failings of the person.

- **The hero gate** — open, participatory, beautiful

- **"Be abundant"** — the most philosophically aligned principle
- **The portal section** — a question, not a claim
- **"What if that wasn't luck?"** — a question that invites reflection
- **"This is my passionate project. A gift."** — Caro's most humble, most compelling moment
- **The Inspired By list** — aspirational without being hierarchical

## The Deepest Question

The feedback's point 7 is the one that lingers longest: Does Dream House help people impose themselves on the future based on what they perceive to be broken in the present? Or does it help people sit with the present — including their own complicity in it — and discover what wants to emerge?

The current copy leans heavily toward the first. The changes above try to open space for the second, without losing the first entirely. Because both are needed. Agency without acceptance becomes tyranny. Acceptance without agency becomes passivity. The most honest version of Dream House holds both.

The copy doesn't need to resolve this tension. It just needs to acknowledge that it exists.
